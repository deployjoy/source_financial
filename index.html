Business Overview

Source Financial is an Australian mortgage brokerage firm specialising in residential property loans. The firm processes approximately 40 loan submissions monthly, serving a diverse client base across Australia. Their core business involves facilitating property financing across various specialised segments including:

  • First home buyers
  • Property investors
  • Self-Managed Super Fund (SMSF) property purchases
  • Self-employed borrowers
  • Trust structure purchases

The business operates under a structured workflow that begins with initial client contact through to settlement, with commission revenue generated through both upfront and trailing commissions from lenders. Revenue is subject to clawback provisions, with specific terms varying by lender, making client retention and relationship management critical to the business model.

The brokerage operates through an aggregator relationship with Specialist Finance Group (SFG), which provides essential infrastructure including the Salestrekker CRM system. This relationship defines certain operational requirements, particularly around compliance documentation and data management.

Key operational characteristics include:

  • High-touch client service model with multiple client touchpoints
  • Comprehensive documentation requirements.
  • Multi-stage assessment and approval process
  • Focus on long-term client relationships to protect trailing commission revenue
  • Hybrid onshore/offshore operational model for document processing.

Key Findings
Operational Strengths
  1. Structured Data Collection
    • Well-defined document request system through Salestrekker integrations.
    • Clear workflow progression from fact-finding to application
    • Systematic client portal usage
    • Standardised initial information gathering
  1. Process Documentation & Control
    • Detailed workflow documentation for each stage in the Workflow Bible Spreadsheet
    • Clear task ownership and timelines
    • Well-defined roles and responsibilities
    • Multiple verification points for quality control
  1. Client Relationship Management
    • Structured follow-up program
    • Multiple touchpoints during application process
    • Quick initial response to leads
    • Clear communication at key milestones
Pain Points & Bottlenecks
  1. System Integration & Data Management
    • Incomplete data mapping between Salestrekker and Apply Online (only 60-70% of data is populated, remainder is filled by Hannah or the offshore team)
    • Manual data transfer between Quickly and Salestrekker
    • Analyst needs to update information in three different places (Salestrekker, Quickly, spreadsheets)
    • Manual screenshots required to document lender email communications in Salestrekker
    • High administrative burden on brokers post-call to record meeting notes and enter data in required systems.
  1. Document Processing Challenges
    • Complex validation requirements varying by lender (e.g., payslips vs. bank statement salary credits)
    • Manual document naming and classification required
    • Manual verification of document accuracy and data extraction
    • Time-consuming document review and compliance note creation
    • Incomplete or incorrect document submissions from clients
    • Time consuming follow up emails and calls to clients to collect more / different documents.
  1. Communication & Notification Management
    • Lender communications coming through multiple channels (emails, back channels)
    • No centralized notification system
    • Important messages can be lost in notification overflow
    • Manual tracking of pre-approval expiries and settlement dates through Outlook calendar
    • Manual task creation and assignment
Risk Areas
  1. Task Management & Prioritization
    • Limited ability to prioritize tasks in Salestrekker
    • No distinction between urgent and routine tasks
    • Manual tracking of critical dates (settlements, pre-approval expiries)
    • No automated daily task rundown
    • Difficulty in managing task delegation and tracking
  1. Process Efficiency
    • Analyst time consumed by manual, repetitive data validation
    • Variable processing times based on client complexity
    • Manual payment collection for CoreLogic valuation reports
    • Multiple team members involved in follow up document collection
  1. Information Accessibility
    • Heavy reliance on analyst's knowledge of lender requirements
    • Manual tracking of application status across different channels
    • Limited visibility of client or deal status. For example, a broker will start working on a deal only to realise it is missing documents.
  1. Revenue Protection
    • Commission clawback exposure
    • Manual commission tracking
    • No way to verify client offset account for commission reconciliation.
    • Manual pre-approval expiry tracking
  1. Resource Allocation
    • High-value staff time spent on administrative tasks
    • Broker time consumed by post-call documentation
    • Analyst involvement in routine document verification
    • Multiple touch points required for basic tasks
Recommendations

Following a comprehensive analysis of Source Financial's operations, drawing from detailed workflow documentation, operational interviews, and process observations, we have identified eight key recommendations. These recommendations are designed to address critical pain points, streamline operations, and leverage modern technology to automate manual processes.

The recommendations have been developed with careful consideration of the technical feasibility of integration with existing systems. Research into Salestrekker’s API capabilities confirms that it supports third-party integrations and data synchronisation, making these recommendations technically viable. The API allows for automated data uploads and downloads, task creation and contact management, which are essential for many of the proposed solutions.


The recommendations focus on three main areas: reducing administrative burden through AI-powered automation, improving system integration and visibility, and implementing quick wins for immediate operational gains. Each recommendation has been evaluated based on implementation complexity, time to deploy, and potential return on investment to help prioritize implementation.
